Canadian field vegetable market · 2024

Interactive dashboard derived from AAFC Statistical Overview of the Canadian Field Vegetable Industry, 2024 and Statistics Canada Table 32-10-0365-01.

Executive summary · BLUF

The five highest farm gate value field crops are dry onions, carrots, tomatoes, cabbage, and lettuce. Canada remains import-dependent: 2024 field vegetable imports exceeded $4.38B vs. exports ~$1.06B (~$3.32B deficit). Lettuce, onions/shallots, tomatoes, and cabbages rank among the largest import lines by value share. Sweet corn is 4th by tonnes but 6th by FGV—outside the value-based top five.

Strategy insights

Jobs-to-be-done (JTBD) — expand each card for grower rationale.

T Field tomatoes

JTBD: Processor contract stability — ~93% of Canadian field tomatoes go to processing. Ontario concentration supports scale logistics; US remains ~96.8% of export value for field vegetables.

C Carrots

JTBD: National footprint + export orientation — ON/QC/NS triad; carrots led ~10.5% share of field vegetable export value in 2024.

O Dry onions

JTBD: Price cycle + geographic diversification — strong 2024 FGV (+12.9% YoY) and volume (+8.0%); AB/QC/ON spread hedges weather and disease pockets.

B Cabbage

JTBD: Dual pathway — domestic + export growth; cabbage export values +18.1% YoY; aligns with ethnic / fermented / slaw demand.

L Lettuce

JTBD: High FGV per tonne but structurally import-backed — lettuce ~16.3% of field vegetable import value; domestic production does not solve off-season / Western retail alone.

Friction points

  • ·Climate / regional volatility: Eastern Canada rebounded from wet 2023; BC and Manitoba saw weaker vegetable performance in national accounts; AAFC cites BC field veg production −8.3% and Manitoba −3.3% vs 2023.
  • ·Trade: Large deficit; Mexico ~24% of import value, US ~59.5% — currency, logistics, tariffs hit lettuce and tomato lines first.
  • ·Operations: Greenhouse is capital- and energy-intensive; Ontario ~72% of national greenhouse tonnage.

White space

Organic / IPM brassicas & carrots in Atlantic + QC corridors.
Year-round leaf & blend SKUs (greenhouse + regional field) vs US/Mexico lettuce share.
Processing tomato genetics for moisture/heat volatility without sacrificing solids.
Onion varieties for storage & energy-stressed curing in Alberta (~13% of national onion production).

Strategy gap · one wedge per crop

Dry onions

Alberta storage onion + export-grade seed stack — AB’s 13% share as distinct day-length / storage / freight sub-market; onion export values +9.9% YoY.

Carrots

Export value recovery — production +1.8% but export values −8.3%; SKU-level quality (sizing, skin, disease) to recapture US retail.

Tomatoes

Split narrative: field processing (~93%) vs greenhouse fresh — coordinated variety planning across two profit pools.

Cabbage

Export-led brassica program for kimchi / sauerkraut / specialty slaw; cabbage also 6.3% of import value.

Lettuce

BC + import substitution CEA play — lettuce leads import share; national weakness in BC vegetables.
