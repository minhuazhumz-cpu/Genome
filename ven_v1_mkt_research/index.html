Bottom line up front

  • ●Scale: Largest documented FAOSTAT lines (2024, via Our World in Data): potatoes ~521 kt, tomatoes ~184 kt, sweet potatoes ~23 kt. Cassava (~453 kt) is a roots/tubers staple, not counted in “vegetables primary.”
  • ●System: National vegetable crop production ~1.29 Mt (2024) (FAOSTAT aggregate). USDA: ~60% of food supply from imports; $3.02 bn ag imports in 2024.
  • ●FGV: Harmonized crop-level farm-gate value in USD not retrieved for 2024–2026—use import-parity and input-cost context (GIEWS, USDA tables).
  • ●White space: Protected cultivation genetics, short-cycle varieties, onion/pepper systems with opaque national series but strong retail pull.

Strategic lenses

Profit pool

Import-parity consumer pricing vs. input-cost drag (diesel, seed, credit). Downstream cold chain & retail may capture more USD-equivalent value than farm gate.

Friction

GIEWS: input costs, erratic rainfall, flooding risk (e.g. Portuguesa). Macro/FX and sanctions risk to import capacity (USDA 2025 outlook).

Strategy insights — Jobs to be done

Expand each card for grower motivations and system context.

Climate & risk — why short cycles win ▼
Erratic rainfall and flood risk in core states (FAO GIEWS) favor short-cycle crops and varietal resilience. Tomato and potato offer faster cash-turn than tree crops.
Cash flow & offtake ▼
High-velocity vegetables align with urban wholesale channels. Policy-linked distribution (e.g. historical CLAP-type programs per USDA food-security narrative) has shaped predictable demand for calorie staples and oils—positioning for complementary horticulture SKUs matters.
Input stack & technology job ▼
Growers “hire” seed and crop protection to reduce variance under expensive diesel and constrained credit (GIEWS). Bundled protocols (genetics + agronomy + storage) outperform commodity bag price selling.
Import parity as ceiling ▼
When ~60% of food supply is import-backed, domestic producers benchmark against landed cost and quality. Seed value props should articulate yield stability and grade-out vs. import substitute.
